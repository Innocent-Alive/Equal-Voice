What LGBTQ+ Stands For ?

" LESBIAN "

A woman whose enduring physical, romantic, and/or emotional attraction is to other women.
Some lesbians may prefer to identify as gay or as gay women.

" GAY "

The adjective describes people whose enduring physical, romantic, and/or emotional attractions are to people of the same sex. Sometimes lesbian is the preferred term for women.

" BISEXUAL "

Bisexual indicates an attraction to all genders. The recognition of bisexual individuals is important, since there have been periods when people who identify as bi have been misunderstood as being gay.

" TRANSGENDER "

Transgender is a term that indicates that a person's gender identity is different from the gender associated with the sex they were assigned at birth.

" QUEER "

queer may be used by people as a specific identity, it is often considered an umbrella term for anyone who is non-cisgender or heterosexual.
But it is also a slur.

" plus(+) "

The 'plus' is used to signify all of the gender identities and sexual orientations that are not specifically covered by the other five initials.
An example is Two-Spirit, a pan-Indigenous American identity.

What Gender Equity Means ?

Gender equity encompasses equality and fairness for all individuals, regardless of their gender identity or sexual orientation. LGBTQ+ (Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, Transgender, Queer, and other non-heterosexual or non-cisgender) individuals often face unique challenges and discrimination that intersect with gender equity.

Gender equity for LGBTQ+ people is a fundamental human right. It is also essential for economic growth, social development, and peace. By working together, we can create a world where everyone, regardless of gender or sexual orientation, has the opportunity to reach their full potential.

What Is The Matter Of Concern ?

Discrimination and Harassment:

  • LGBTQ+ individuals face higher rates of discrimination and harassment compared to their cisgender and heterosexual counterparts.
  • According to a survey conducted by the Williams Institute, 46% of LGBTQ+ employees reported experiencing discrimination in the workplace.
  • Transgender individuals, in particular, face heightened levels of discrimination and harassment, leading to adverse impacts on their mental health and employment opportunities.

Healthcare Disparities:

  • LGBTQ+ individuals often encounter barriers to accessing inclusive and competent healthcare services.
  • Studies have shown that LGBTQ+ individuals are less likely to seek medical care due to fear of discrimination or receiving inadequate treatment.
  • LGBTQ+ youth face higher rates of mental health issues and suicide attempts compared to their heterosexual and cisgender peers.

Intersectionality:

  • Intersectionality is a crucial consideration when addressing gender equity within LGBTQ+ communities.
  • Individuals who identify as LGBTQ+ and also belong to marginalized groups based on race, ethnicity, socioeconomic status, disability, or immigration status may experience compounded discrimination and face additional barriers.

Legal Rights and Protection:

  • Laws and legal protections for LGBTQ+ individuals vary widely across countries and jurisdictions.
  • Many countries have made significant progress in recognizing and protecting LGBTQ+ rights, including marriage equality and anti-discrimination laws.
  • However, there are still regions where LGBTQ+ individuals face legal challenges, discrimination, and criminalization.
